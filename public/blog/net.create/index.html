Net.Create is an open-source analysis tool that allows for the simultaneous building of a network by multiple users. The ability to create a network simultaneously with other researchers gives Net.Create the ability to foster creativity and responsiveness within a research team. Using metadata from the reading selections that we have worked on this semester, our digital history class used Net.Create to craft a network that represented the interconnected nature of these readings.

First, we made note of the metadata for each of our assigned readings. This included not only a bibliographic citation and DOI, but also possibly a summary or abstract of the reading. The information was then used to construct nodes and edges in Net.Create. Our nodes were the authors of our assigned readings, and the edges were the interconnectivity between theses authors. Perhaps they were connected by joint authorship or subject. The end result was a visual reminder that many of our readings overlapped areas of scholarship and were more connected to each other than we realized.
